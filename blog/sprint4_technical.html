Problem Solving

When I got blocked on a simple problem...

I was tired and I knew it. I had been staring at the same problem why was my capitalize function returning the words twice, once capsed and once original? and cycling round the same options of solutions that I already knew didn't work for a good 10 minutes or so. I was feeling irked.
That's when it happened.
My timer went off, I woke up slightly from my code filled daze, drank some water and went for a jog down the street and back. On my arrival back home I felt alive and energised, ready to go. I sat back at my desk, set the timer again and asked myself what I was going to do by the time it went off again. The answer was this: google my problem and read carefully so I understand, look at a few different sites and try the things out. If no luck still then ask my question on Slack but before sending it take a minute to reflect on it, maybe go to the toilet or have more water. If still no answer then post message. I then received some helpful clues and tips to solve the problem. Yay, success!!
I learnt that having a timer on is a wonderful check-in point and way to stop the hours going by so fast. I also learnt that the input to the function was wrong and returning that AND the correct input minus the first letter. Learnt a lot more about what not to do which will come in handy to remind me when I am doing those things again... hopefully.

When I solved a problem in an elegant way...

This morning I woke up. It was sunny and I felt excited to get into studying because during my half slumber time before my alarm went off again, my brain had come up with the solution to the problem I was working on the night before. All I had to do was stop working on it, tuck myself in and have a good old rest, that's my new favourite kind of problem solving technique and look forward to using it again in the future. How did I feel? Well rested, delighted and proud of my brain and all that it does for me. I learnt that leaving my computer and giving myself space to think can be a great way to solve problems.

Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem solving techniques and process:

Pseudocode Okay, 5/10, maybe even a 6. I like that it gives clues about how to solve the test.
Trying something Yup, trying and trying more, I'm all over that, 8/10 for that.
Rubber ducky method I've been talking to my drink bottle and outloud to myself, has been working well for me. When my flatmate came out of his room he was confused as he swore I was having a conversation with someone but no one else was there. I explained myself, don't worry.
Reading error messages hum, maybe a 3.5/10. Sometimes I just don't know what it is saying or where that extra curly bracket is.
Console.logging Oh console.log my friend who sees what's actually going on so well. Feeling well acquainted and confident, 10/10.
Googling 8/10, I am getting better at refining my question and finding the pages that will give me the info I need relatively quickly. Could definitely get better at all of these things.
Asking your peers for help Have been doing this, even my flatmate who is interested but has no coding experience. Could ask more or quicker, I don't want to ask all the time and I want myself to get the answer and do it myself.
Asking coaches for help Have done this a few times. Very useful especially over a video call as Jo$eph explains it well and I can ask small questions to make sure I'm understanding it correctly.
